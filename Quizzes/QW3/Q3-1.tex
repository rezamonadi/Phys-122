\textbf{Problem:} \hfill (20 points)\\

During a 1 minute, a refrigerator removes an amount of thermal energy of $Q_C = \SI{3.0}{MJ}$
from the interior while using electrical work of $W = \SI{1.5}{MJ}$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \textbf{(5 points)}  
Write the  \textbf{coefficient of performance (COP)} 
for a refrigerator in terms of what you get and what you pay for.

\vspace{2cm}

\item[(b)] \textbf{(5 points)}  
Calculate the numerical value of the refrigerator’s \textbf{COP}.

\vspace{3cm}

\item[(c)] \textbf{(5 points)}  
Using conservation of energy, determine the amount of thermal energy
$Q_H$ released into the room during this time interval. Draw a diagram.

\vspace{3cm}

\item[(d)] \textbf{(5 points)}  
Explain briefly (one or two sentences) why the COP of a refrigerator
can be greater than 1 without violating conservation of energy.

\vspace{2cm}
\end{enumerate}
